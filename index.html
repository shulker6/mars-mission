Oxygen production
Mars's 95% CO₂ atmosphere is fuel for cyanobacteria (Anabaena, Spirulina, Chlorella), which photosynthesize inside bioreactors — turning astronauts' exhaled CO₂ into breathable O₂.
Water recycling
NASA's MELiSSA project uses bacteria like Nitrosomonas to recover up to 95% of wastewater biologically — eliminating the need to launch tonnes of water from Earth.
Growing food
Martian regolith is toxic to plants. Rhizobium fixes atmospheric nitrogen; mycorrhizal fungi boost root uptake; microbes neutralize perchlorates — together transforming sterile soil into farmland.
